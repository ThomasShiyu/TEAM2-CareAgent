# AgenticCare

**Group 2 — Senior Care Coordination**

An agentic AI solution built for the CREATE-a-Thon, exploring how autonomous AI
agents can support older adults living independently — while taking the social
and ethical dimensions of SE4AI seriously.

---

## Problem

Older adults living independently often struggle with:

- Medication management
- Appointments
- Transportation
- Social isolation

These challenges compound one another: a missed medication can lead to a missed
appointment, and social isolation makes it harder to notice when something is
wrong.

## Agentic AI Angle

An AI care assistant could:

- **Coordinate reminders** — medications, appointments, and daily routines
- **Contact caregivers** — reach family members or care staff when needed
- **Detect anomalies** — flag unusual patterns in activity or wellness data
- **Arrange transportation** — book rides to appointments and errands
- **Summarize medical instructions** — translate complex guidance into plain language
- **Monitor wellness check-ins** — regular, low-friction touchpoints with the person

## Ethical Issues

We treat these as first-class design constraints, not afterthoughts:

- **Consent and autonomy** — the older adult remains in control of what the system does and shares
- **Surveillance concerns** — monitoring must support, not spy on, the person
- **Accessibility** — the interface must work for varied physical and cognitive abilities
- **False alarms** — anomaly detection must minimize alert fatigue and unnecessary panic
- **Digital literacy barriers** — the system should not assume comfort with technology

## About the CREATE-a-Thon

This project was created as part of the CREATE-a-Thon, a group activity
challenging trainees to build an agentic solution for an assigned community
problem area while weighing the social and ethical aspects of SE4AI.

**Group 2's assigned problem area:** Senior Care Coordination.
